Academia

Since then I have held visiting (postdoc) positions at University of Arkansas (3 years), James Madison University (1 year), and Mount Holyoke College (3 years).

Teaching

Over the past 9 years, plus the time when I was in grad school, I have taught calc I-III, differential equations, survey of calculus (light calculus course at Arkansas), discrete math, group theory, ring theory, linear algebra with differential equations (JMU), intro and intermediate linear algebra, and commutative algebra (grad level). I also taught a GRE prep course for the quantitaive reasoning portion of the exam, the summer after I graduated college.

You can see some of my old teaching websites and materials by going to the ArkansasWebsite folder in my GitHub repository, AcademiaMaterials. For more recent materials, I don't think I had a website at JMU, and even though I did have a website at MHC it's gone now, and most of my materials were posted on Moodle, anyway. At Georgia Tech I only created teaching websites for the SY 2021-2022 to post my recorded lectures (and now that Georgia Tech doesn't use BlueJeans anymore, the platform I used to make the videos, the lectures are no longer available).
